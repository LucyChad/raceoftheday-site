Willie Mullins trains from Closnamore in County Carlow, Ireland. The yard has grown from a respected Irish jumping operation to the dominant force across the full spectrum of jump racing in Britain and Ireland. The scale is unmatched - in the 2026 Grand National alone, Mullins ran eight of the 34 horses in the field. That level of representation is without precedent in the modern era of the race.

The operation is a family business in the fullest sense. His son Patrick (Mr P W Mullins) rides regularly, including on Grangeclare West in the 2026 National. His brother Tom trains from a separate yard. The Mullins name carries weight throughout racing on both sides of the Irish Sea.

The 2026 Grand National

I Am Maximus, a ten-year-old French-bred chaser rated 168, won the 2026 Randox Grand National at 9/2 under Paul Townend. Mullins' stable jockey completed the ride with the composure that defines his partnership with Mullins. The horse had been well-prepared for the race and justified favouritism on Good to Soft ground in a 34-runner field.

Mullins ran eight horses. Won with the favourite. Had the 5th (66/1). Two others unseated. The race was shaped by his yard.

Grangeclare West, sent off at 9/1 and widely considered a genuine challenger, unseated Patrick Mullins during the race. Had he completed, the Mullins yard would have dominated the first two positions. That result will frame how the 2027 market for Grangeclare West is approached.

Paul Townend - the key jockey relationship

Townend has been Mullins' retained jockey since the retirement of Ruby Walsh. The partnership has produced championship success at Cheltenham, Punchestown, and now Aintree. Townend's style suits the Mullins horses - patient in the early stages, confident in the finishing stretch. When Mullins and Townend combine at a major festival, the combination demands respect regardless of the price.

Cheltenham and Punchestown - the festival record

Mullins has transformed the Cheltenham Festival from a predominantly British event into one where Irish dominance is expected rather than notable. The Racing API data shows 221 runners at Cheltenham across 2024-2026, producing 27 winners at a 12% strike rate and +69.49 level-stake profit. An A/E of 0.89 tells you the market is aware of his Cheltenham potency — it prices his runners slightly short of where they actually perform.

Punchestown in April is the second major festival target, where Mullins races on home soil against strong Irish competition. His dominance there is even more pronounced than at Cheltenham.

What Mullins' dominance means for betting

When Mullins enters a race with multiple horses, the race shape changes. Secondaries from his yard can run for each other, setting pace and making life difficult for rivals. Single-runner races against a Mullins horse require careful assessment of which stable jockey is booked - Townend aboard signals first-string.

His novice hurdlers and chasers warrant attention early in the season when the market has not yet assessed them fully. His festival horses are rarely available at value by the time the meeting arrives. The opportunity is in the autumn and winter, before the market fully prices in what Closnamore is preparing.
