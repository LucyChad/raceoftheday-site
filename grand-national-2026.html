The winner: I Am Maximus

I Am Maximus came into this renewal with strong credentials - a top-rated chaser rated 168, well-proven over fences, and with Willie Mullins and Paul Townend providing the most powerful team in jump racing. At 9/2, he was sent off favourite in a race where favourites have a poor record historically. He justified that market confidence to win from Iroko and Jordans.

The ten-year-old was produced to challenge at the right time by Townend, who has ridden the horse throughout his career at Mullins' yard. A horse rated 168 carrying a competitive weight - the market had him right.

Mullins' dominance: eight runners, one winner and a second

Willie Mullins ran eight horses in the 2026 Grand National. I Am Maximus won. High Class Hero (66/1) finished fifth. Grangeclare West (9/1) and Captain Cody, Lecky Watson, Spanish Harlem, and Quai De Bourbon all exited the race without completing.

Mullins ran 8 of the 34 runners. Nearly one in four horses in the race came from Closnamore.

That level of representation changes how the race is read before and after. When a trainer fields a quarter of the field, the question of which one wins matters less than whether the stable wins at all. Grangeclare West unseating was the significant result from a market perspective - he was sent off at 9/1 and was widely considered the trainer's best chance alongside the winner.

The faller that shaped the market: Panic Attack

Dan Skelton's Panic Attack was sent off at 7/1 - second favourite - and fell during the race. Harry Skelton was aboard a horse many expected to challenge for the first three. The fall left the market leaderboard without its second-strongest contender and opened the door for Iroko and Jordans to fill the places at 18/1 and 28/1 respectively.

Panic Attack had run well in staying chase company prior to Aintree and represented the leading British handler's best chance at the race. The Skelton yard will return.

The places: Iroko and Jordans

Iroko, trained by the Greenall and Guerriero partnership and ridden by Jonjo O'Neill Jr, finished a clear second at 18/1. The eight-year-old had been lightly campaigned and handled the demands of the National course well. His stablemate Jagwar (17/2) unseated Mark Walsh - Greenall and Guerriero will feel that run differently had Jagwar completed.

Jordans in third at 28/1, trained by Joseph O'Brien and ridden by Ben Jones, was a notable result. A seven-year-old finishing third in the Grand National is unusual - the race traditionally favours older horses with Aintree experience. Jordans ran to his rating and beyond.

What the result means for next year

I Am Maximus will be eleven in 2027 - an age at which Grand National winners are rare but not unknown. His current mark of 168 makes him difficult to weight out of contention under the handicapper. Grangeclare West, if he runs, will be a more experienced Aintree participant. Panic Attack - assuming Skelton brings him back - remains a live contender at whatever mark the handicapper settles on.

The Greenall and Guerriero stable, with Iroko and Jagwar both having strong cases for next year's field, will approach the 2027 entries with genuine confidence.
