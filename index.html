The man behind the selections

Frank Chadwick has been attending and analysing race meetings since the 1960s. He is not a data analyst or a software system. He is a horseman — someone who understands why a horse runs better on soft ground at Haydock than on good at Cheltenham, and what a trainer's pattern of entries tells you before the market has priced it in.

The discipline that defines his approach is what he doesn't bet on. Roughly nine races in ten are filtered out before he considers a selection. The ones that remain — where the form, ground, draw, trainer pattern and price align — are the races Race Of The Day members receive.

Every selection Frank makes is logged at the time it is made, before the race runs, with the advised price. Every result is recorded against it. The record is open to members before they subscribe. There is no selective memory and no cherry-picked period.
