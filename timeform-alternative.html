Why Timeform Jury Daily closed - and why that does not apply here.

The Jury Daily problem
Advised prices became unachievable
Timeform cited "challenges faced with rapidly changing prices" as the reason for closure. Tips were published at 11:15am with specific bookmaker odds. By the time subscribers acted, those prices had moved. The service could not deliver what it promised.
Why ROTD is different
We do not advise prices. We advise selections.
Race Of The Day tells you which races are worth betting and which are not. We do not tell you to back a horse at 5/2 with a specific bookmaker. You find your own price. That sidesteps the exact mechanism that made Jury Daily untenable.
The methodology
Systematic. Non-personality-led. Forty years of experience.
The intelligence behind Race Of The Day comes from someone with four decades of reading form and studying markets. He does not do interviews. The selections are the product - not the person. That is the same philosophy Jury Daily operated on, without any of the structural risks.
Transparency
The full record is open before you pay.
Twelve months of selections. Every result. Every month - including the flat ones. Published on the site and accessible before you subscribe. We are not going to make you look for the bad months.
