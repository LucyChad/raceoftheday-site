Hawk Mountain - the O'Brien and Moore combination

The strongest profile in the field belongs to Hawk Mountain. Trained by Aidan O'Brien and ridden by Ryan Moore, he arrives having won three of his last four starts. The form figure of 5111 shows a horse who started slowly as a juvenile and has improved steadily since. Moore choosing to partner him over stablemates is always significant at this level.

The Rowley Mile suits a horse with a strong galloping action. O'Brien's juveniles often do their best work stepping up in trip and company in the spring, and the Craven has served that purpose well historically.

Hidden Force - Appleby's unbeaten contender

Hidden Force represents the Godolphin operation trained by Charlie Appleby, with William Buick in the saddle. Two runs, two wins - the 1-1 form figure masks a horse who has not been fully tested yet. Appleby and Buick have won this race before and arrive with a horse whose potential remains largely unknown.

Hidden Force has not been beaten. Two runs, two wins. The question is whether those wins came against suitable opposition.

Avicenna - Varian's unbeaten two-year-old

Roger Varian sends Avicenna in with a 100 per cent record from two starts. Ray Dawson rides. Varian has a strong record with well-bred three-year-olds at Newmarket and Avicenna looks the type who improves as the season develops. Whether two wins as a juvenile translate to Group 3 company on the Rowley Mile is what today establishes.

The case for Venetian Prince and Commander's Intent

Venetian Prince (Andrew Balding, PJ McDonald) has placed twice since a convincing win, finishing second on both occasions. A form figure of 61-22 shows a horse who has been placed against decent company without winning. Balding's horses often improve significantly as three-year-olds through the spring.

Commander's Intent (George Scott, James Doyle) has been placed five times without winning. A form figure full of twos is the mark of a consistent horse who has not yet found a race he can win at this level. Doyle takes the ride.

Oxagon and Hankelow

Oxagon carries the Gosden flag with Oisin Murphy. Form of 51255 suggests a horse at the limit of his ability at this level. Hankelow from K R Burke arrives having won twice from three starts, but the draw in stall six makes life harder on a track where low numbers can prove advantageous.

Race Of The Day - Craven Stakes Assessment

Three horses dominate the form book. Hawk Mountain has the strongest profile on figures and brings the Moore factor in a race where the leading trainer-jockey combination matters. Hidden Force is unbeaten and represents the division's most powerful operation. Avicenna is the unknown quantity - two wins from two but not yet tested at this level. A race that will define the Classic order for several of these horses going forward.

The Abernant Stakes - also today at Newmarket (3:00)

The Zyn Abernant Stakes (Group 3, 6f, Good) earlier on the card at 3:00 is worth attention for different reasons. An eight-year-old in Diligent Harry (Clive Cox, Rossa Ryan) arrives in form having won his last two starts, form reading 150-11. At 8, he is at the age where sprinters can maintain their best form for several seasons without physical deterioration.

Elmonjed (William Haggas, Tom Marquand) is the consistent option on form of 21021. Beckford's Folly (Charlie Appleby, William Buick) is a three-year-old testing Group 3 company for the first time having shown useful juvenile form.
